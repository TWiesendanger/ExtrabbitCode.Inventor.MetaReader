Sometimes you don't just want to *look* at a model - you want to point at something. **Redlining**
lets you draw directly over the 3D view: circle a problem area, add an arrow and a note, or paint
right onto the geometry. Everything you draw lives on **layers** that are stored alongside the
cached viewable, so your markup is still there the next time the model opens.

<ThemedImage slug="app__redline" alt="Redlining on the fishing-reel sample: a circle, an arrow and a 3D paint stroke, with the layers panel open" />

Click the **pencil** in the viewer toolbar (or press **E**) to start; press **Esc** or the **✕** in
the palette to leave. Markup never changes the model - it's an overlay. Redlining is available on
Inventor parts and assemblies (both [engines](/docs/3d-view)); STEP views don't have it yet.

## Tools

The palette at the top gives you, from left to right:

- **Freehand** (`F`) - sketch lines in screen space.
- **Paint on model** (`P`) - the 3D pen; see below.
- **Shapes** - a dropdown with **rectangle**, **circle** and **arrow**; the button shows the shape
  you used last.
- **Text** - click anywhere and type; click elsewhere to commit.
- **Eraser** - click or drag across any markup to remove it, 2D or 3D.
- **Colour** and **stroke width** - two dropdowns; the colour swatch and dot preview your current
  pick. The width applies to both 2D strokes and the 3D pen.
- **Screenshot** - export the current view including markup: **save as PNG** or **copy to the
  clipboard**, optionally stamped with an **info block** (file name, layer, date).
- **Undo** (`Ctrl+Z`) - steps back through draws *and* erases, in order, across all layers.

To orbit or zoom while redlining, just pick any of the viewer's own navigation tools (or press
**O**) - drawing pauses, and the palette stays put. Pick a drawing tool to continue.

## Painting on the model

The **3D pen** is different from the other tools: instead of drawing on the screen, it paints
**onto the surface of the model itself**. Strokes hug the geometry, stay attached when you orbit or
zoom, and are hidden by parts in front of them - like marker on the actual part. The stroke width
is taken from the width dropdown at the moment you start drawing.

Because 3D paint lives on the model, it reads well from any camera angle. The 2D tools (freehand,
shapes, text) draw in screen space instead - they line up with the model only from the camera pose
they were drawn in, which is exactly what layers store.

## Layers

The **Layers** panel docks on the right while redlining is active. Each layer keeps its own markup
and remembers the **camera pose** it was created with:

- **＋** adds a layer; the newest markup always goes to the **selected** layer.
- **Double-click** a layer to rename it.
- The **eye** hides or shows a layer (3D paint included).
- The **camera** button restores the layer's saved view - so 2D markup lines up again; the **pin**
  re-saves the current view to the layer.
- The **trash** deletes a layer and everything on it.

Drag the panel's left edge to resize it.

## Saved with the viewable

Markup is persisted automatically - a few moments after you stop drawing, all layers (including 3D
paint, per stroke colour and width) are written next to the cached viewable. Close the model, reopen
it next week: your layers are back.

<Callout type="warn">
  Markup is stored **in the 3D cache entry** of the model. If you clear the viewables cache in
  **Settings → 3D Viewer**, the markup goes with it.
</Callout>

## Keyboard shortcuts

`E` toggles redlining, `F`/`P` pick the pens, `O` switches to navigation, `C` toggles body
coloring. All of these are **rebindable**: click the **keyboard** button in the viewer toolbar to
open the shortcuts window, click a key, and press its replacement. A key that's already taken is
refused, and **Reset to defaults** brings everything back. Your bindings are remembered like any
other setting.
